JoinIEEE

What are the benefits of joining?

  • It's a good way to connect with other EE/CompE/CS students who will be in similar classes and share similar interests
  • Benefit from the insight provided at meetings: our IEEE chapter is focused on helping students become leaders in industry by providing them with insights into what engineers in our field are doing. Our meetings feature guest speakers from various corporations who are seeking the brightest and most talented engineers
  • Membership includes subscriptions to IEEE Spectrum and IEEE Potentials
  • Be able to participate in IEEE contests to win scholarships and awards

Who should join IEEE?

  • Membership is open to both undergraduate and graduate students
  • Those who "carry at least 50% of a normal full-time academic program as a registered undergraduate or graduate student in a regular course of study in IEEE-designated fields" (from the IEEE website)
  • Generally, you must be an Electrical, Computer Engineering, or Computer Sciene major. However, as long as you have interest in new and innovative technologies related to IEEE, then you may join even if you are of another major. Especially if you are getting some type of related minor, then joining the IEEE TAMU chapter is highly recommended.

How do I join?

  • Register with the National IEEE. Be sure to check that you are applying for student membership and that you select Texas A&M University - College Station as your student branch. Don't forget to save your member ID after registration.
  • Sign up for our listserv on the left navigation bar! We use this list to notify you of upcoming events and opportunities.
  • There are NO local branch dues and national dues are only $32 (full-time employess pay $181 per year so take advantage of this).
